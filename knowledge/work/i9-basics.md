# I-9 basics — starter quick reference

> Starter summary only. Verify everything against the current M-274 on

## What Form I-9 is

Form I-9 (Employment Eligibility Verification) verifies the identity and
employment authorization of every person hired for employment in the United
States. Every employer must complete one for every new hire, citizen or not.

## Core deadlines

- Section 1 (employee): completed no later than the first day of employment.
- Section 2 (employer): completed within 3 business days of the employee's
  first day of employment. If the job lasts fewer than 3 days, Section 2 must
  be done by the first day.

## Acceptable documents

- List A — proves BOTH identity and employment authorization (e.g., U.S.
  passport, Permanent Resident Card, EAD with photo, foreign passport with
  I-551 stamp or I-94 for certain statuses).
- List B — proves identity only (e.g., driver's license, state ID).
- List C — proves employment authorization only (e.g., unrestricted Social
  Security card, birth certificate).
- Employee presents either ONE List A document, OR one List B + one List C.
- Employer may not specify which documents an employee must present
  (document abuse is prohibited).

## Reverification

- Required when an employee's employment authorization or documentation
  expires (Supplement B, formerly Section 3).
- Never reverify U.S. citizens, or permanent residents who presented a
  Permanent Resident Card. Never reverify List B identity documents.

## Retention

- Retain I-9 for 3 years after date of hire OR 1 year after employment ends,
  whichever is later.

## Common pitfalls

- Accepting expired documents (not allowed, with narrow exceptions).
- Backdating — never. Late completion is documented honestly.
- Specifying documents or demanding "the green card" — document abuse.
- Missing reverification dates for time-limited authorizations.
